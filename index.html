What is Power Ten?

In rowing, Power Ten is an order by the coxswain for the crew to go to maximum effort to achieve victory. To our team, Power Ten epitomizes total coordination and focus of our resources and partners to achieve maximum success.

Power Ten’s mission is to deliver top tier investment returns by investing, creating, and managing a diversified portfolio of market disruptive technologies that can be accelerated via a highly efficient development process to maximize value and minimize risk.

We believe that building and leveraging strong partnerships is the optimal way to create sustainable and valuable health technology companies

Our Philosophy

  • Be Focused – Focus on investments in disruptive technologies that meet large pains in the market via early and growth stage opportunities, as well as licensing from world leading entrepreneurs and scientists.
  • Seek Leverage – Use our strategic partner network to validate feasibility, technology development, regulatory pathway, and market push-pull to achieve investment validation and success.
  • Drive to the Finish – Provide direction, knowledge, and guidance to our companies through our strategic resources to achieve maximum efficiency to realize our investment goals.

Our Team

Management

Norman Stapleton

Norman Stapleton is the founder of Power Ten Ventures. He serves as Managing Partner for Power Ten Medical Ventures I, LP, the predicate fund to Power Ten Ventures II, LP. Norman is a former naval officer serving as an engineering officer onboard USS Champion (MCM-4). After transitioning out of the military, Mr. Stapleton worked in management positions at International Paper, PSDI, and Texas Instruments. While at Texas Instruments, Norman was Director of Medical Strategy and was the architect of TI’s largest equity investment into a private entity which led to the establishment of TI’s medical business unit. In 2007, Norman left TI and began consulting in Israel for medical device startups, and was integral in the establishment of Rainbow Medical, Ltd., Israel’s leading medical device development company, where he serves on the Investment Committee. In 2010, Norman launched Power Ten Medical Ventures I, LP, which has already attained a substantial return on investment for its limited partners.

Norman is a 1991 and 1992 Intercollegiate Rowing Association Champion. He is a member of the U.S. Naval Academy Athletic Hall of Fame with the 1992 Varsity Heavyweight Eight. He was also awarded the 1992 Rusty Callow trophy which is given to that member of the Varsity Heavyweight Crew who contributed most to the team’s determination, competitive desire, and willingness to sacrifice.

Norman holds a Bachelor of Science degree from the United States Naval Academy, a Master of Business Administration from Carnegie Mellon University, and a Master of Engineering in Biomedical Engineering from Cornell University.

Craig Gomulka

Craig has been a venture investor in healthcare and technology for over 16 years and currently serves as a Venture Partner and advisor for Power Ten Ventures. Most recently Craig served as Vice President of Strategic Investments at Highmark Health and as Managing Director of Highmark Ventures, where he led Highmark’s corporate venture capital efforts. Before joining Highmark, Craig served as the Chief Investment Officer of Innovation Works, a Pittsburgh-based development fund where he executed over 60 new investments and follow-on financings and managed through 12 portfolio company acquisitions. Prior to Innovation Works, Craig was a Director and Partner with Draper Triangle Ventures, an early stage healthcare and IT focused venture capital firm with $185M under management. Craig has served on the boards of Axial Healthcare, BitArmor Systems (acq. by Trustwave), Imago Scientific (acq. by AMETEK), and Hypernex (acq. by Nova Measuring Instruments, Inc. (NASDAQ: NVMI)), and as a board observer for Acclaris (acq. by Towers Watson) and Array Health Solutions.

Prior to joining Draper Triangle, Craig worked for Intel Corporation where he built supplier pricing software and with RRZ Investment Management as a software developer, designing and programming fixed income and equity trading software and algorithms.

Craig received his B.A. from Colgate University. Craig achieved his MBA from Carnegie Mellon University Tepper School of Business in May of 2000 and was awarded the Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA) designation in September of 2000.

Advisors

Marie Csete MD, PhD

Dr. Csete is a Scientific Advisor to Power Ten Ventures. Marie received her undergraduate music degree from Princeton University, followed by an MD from Columbia University, College of Physicians & Surgeons. She is board certified in Anesthesiology with subspecialty certification in Critical Care. Her academic career focused on optimizing the perioperative management of liver transplant patients, and she led the liver transplant anesthesiology services at UCSF, UCLA, Michigan and Emory where she was co-director of the MD/PhD program and founding director of the Emory/GaTech human embryonic stem cell core facility. Mid-career Dr. Csete received a PhD in developmental biology from Caltech. Her PhD work focused on the role of oxygen in stem cell fate, which led to later work in stem cell aging. Dr. Csete was the first CSO of CIRM, the California state stem cell agency where she supervised development of its core programs. Currently President & Chief Scientist of the non-profit Huntington Medical Research Institutes, her lab studies iPS models of neurodegeneration. At HMRI she is leading a new scientific strategic plan focused on multidisciplinary approaches to common diseases with core emphasis on inflammation and metabolism, and development of new imaging approaches to quantify these processes. She also serves as Associate Medical Director of Clinical Research at Huntington Memorial Hospital, Professor of Anesthesiology at Keck USC School of Medicine and Visiting Associate, Caltech Medical Engineering.

Farrell O. Mendelsohn, M.D.

Dr. Farrell Mendelsohn is one of the nation’s leading interventional cardiologist and serves as a Scientific Advisor to Power Ten Ventures. He is a partner at Cardiology, PC at the Princeton Heart and Vascular Center in Birmingham, Alabama where he directs the cardiovascular research programs, which includes one of the nation’s leading research sites in the United States for regenerative medicine studies. Dr. Mendelsohn also directs one of the nation’s leading carotid stent programs which has treated more patients that any other program in the Southeast with one of the lowest complication rates in the nation. He currently manages the first in man research study for Vascular Dynamics’ MobiusHD® device, a catheter-delivered carotid implant designed for the treatment of drug resistant hypertension.

Dr. Mendelsohn graduated from Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine and completed his internship and residency at Massachusetts General Hospital. While completing his residency, Dr. Mendelsohn served as a clinical instructor in internal medicine at Harvard Medical School. Dr. Mendelsohn completed a fellowship at Duke University Medical Center in clinical and interventional cardiology with a specialization in the management of patients with vascular disease. He was selected as the first Joseph C. Greenfield, Jr. Scholar in Interventional Cardiology. Dr. Mendelsohn has published articles on carotid, renal, and peripheral arterial stenting and has presented on these topics at national and international scientific meetings. Dr. Mendelsohn has also been extensively involved in the design and development of coronary and catheter-based systems, including the development of the Vessix Renal Denervation system at Boston Scientific where he also serves on the Renal Denervation Scientific Advisory Board. Dr. Mendelsohn is the Chief Medical Officer for InnoMed Scientific in Shanghai, China where he also co-founded Gateway Medical, which was the first pre-clinical commercial laboratory in Asia researching and developing medical device technologies.

Dr. Mendelsohn is Board Certified in Cardiovascular Disease and Interventional Cardiology, Vascular Medicine and Endovascular Medicine. Dr. Mendelsohn is a Fellow of the American College of Cardiology, as well as a member of the Jefferson County Medical Society, the Alabama Medical Association, and the Baptist Health System Foundation Board.
